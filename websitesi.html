Welcome to the Computational Neuroscience Lab

We study brain dynamics with EEG/MEG/fMRI, build computational models, and develop machine learning methods for neural data.

Research

Focus areas and current projects

EEG-based Trust Modeling

Unsupervised learning for trust-related affective states; approach–withdrawal indices; connectivity and graph features.

Neural Signal Processing

Time–frequency analysis, AR models, source localization, and robust denoising for real-world recordings.

Computational Psychiatry

Latent-state modeling for attention, stress, and decision-making under uncertainty.

Teaching

Courses, labs, and materials

Digital Circuits (Verilog)

Project ideas: keypad lock with AND/OR logic only, FSM design, and timing analysis.

Signals & Systems (EEE 309)

Continuous/discrete-time signals; LTI system properties (linearity, time-invariance, causality, stability); convolution; impulse & step responses; Fourier series & transform; sampling & DTFT; Laplace & Z-transforms; MATLAB/Simulink labs.

Syllabus (EEE 309)

Adaptive Signal Processing (EEE 506)

Optimal mean-square estimation; Wiener filters; adaptive structures & least-squares; state-space models and Kalman filters; search techniques (gradient & Newton); LMS & RLS algorithms; analysis of adaptive algorithms (learning curve, convergence, stability, excess MSE, misadjustment); applications to noise cancellation & system identification.
